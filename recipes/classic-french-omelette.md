## Description
The French omelette is about texture: pale yellow, just-set, rolled rather than folded. It rewards a low burner and a steady hand.

## Instructions
1. Whisk eggs and salt thoroughly with a fork until completely uniform — no streaks of white.
2. Heat the butter in an 8-inch nonstick skillet over medium-low until it foams but does not brown.
3. Pour in the eggs. Immediately stir with the fork in small circles while shaking the pan to keep the bottom from setting too fast.
4. When the eggs are mostly set but still wet on top (about 45 seconds), stop stirring. Smooth the top with the spatula.
5. Tilt the pan toward you and use the spatula to fold the far edge into the middle. Roll the omelette out of the pan onto the plate so the seam ends up underneath.
6. Garnish with chives if using.

## Notes
- The pan is too hot if the eggs sizzle on contact.
- Three eggs is the right number — two are too thin, four are unwieldy.

## Substitutions
- Olive oil for butter (changes flavor; still works).
- Fresh tarragon for chives.
