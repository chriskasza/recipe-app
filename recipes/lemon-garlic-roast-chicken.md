## Description
A weekend bird. Dry-brining for an hour beforehand gives the crispest skin; if you have overnight, even better.

## Instructions
1. Pat the chicken dry. Rub all over with salt, pepper, lemon zest, and olive oil. Let sit on a rack at room temperature for 1 hour.
2. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F).
3. Stuff the cavity with the halved lemon, the head of garlic, and the thyme sprigs. Tie the legs together with kitchen twine.
4. Arrange the lemon rounds on the bottom of the roasting pan. Place the chicken on top, breast-side up.
5. Roast for 70–80 minutes, until the thigh reads 74 °C (165 °F).
6. Rest for 15 minutes before carving.

## Notes
- Save the bones for stock. The roasted lemon rounds make a fantastic sauce base — mash a few with the pan drippings.

## Substitutions
- Rosemary or sage for thyme.
- Skinless cuts work but lose the textural payoff.

## Make-ahead
Salt up to 24 hours ahead and rest uncovered in the fridge for the driest skin.
